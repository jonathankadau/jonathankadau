MAPSCAPE

Type Studio, UNCC - Spring 2025

Focus Maps as a process

Group Franklin Bell, Tom Wileman

Professor Peter Wong, Jeffery Balmer

Mapscape is a product of using 3 maps from Rome throughout time to create geometry and space. The program is a space for anyone to come see stored important maps. The project takes up a small piazza in Trastevere, Rome. Our design uses large encasements that create space and float throughout the building — not only holding the maps, but also acting as masses mined out of the maps used in our design process.

The project draws from Neil Denari's early work as well as Bernard Tschumi.

This moment in the project marks a shift from mapping as reference to mapping as generator. The drawings below focus on how diagrammed cartography becomes volumetric enclosure and public space.
